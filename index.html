Resume

  • R&D Engineer

    My engineer degree in Systems, Networks and Telecommunications from the University of Technology of Troyes allowed me the work in R&D. My first experiences with startups gave me the skills to be autonomous on many areas and to translate the clients needs into technical functionnalities.


    From electronics and embedded systems, to docker and CI/CD, adding front-end development (React, Meteor) backend development (NodeJS) and databases design and management (MongoDB, InfluxDB, MySQL), I have the profile of a full-stack developer with engineering mindset.

  • Project Manager

    Teamwork is a must for a well-conducted project. From group projects at the university, to associative events and professionnal projects, my skills in project management are diverse and complete. I master several tools like Teams, Slack, GitHub or Trello.


    My professional experiences gave me the opportunities to manage planning, small teams (5 people) and client relations, including support and formations.

  • Security Expert

    A master in Security of Informations Systems followed by a PhD about the security of embedded systems allowed me to have a complete view on security in information technologies. That is why I include on every projects I work on a security by design approach. I take part in security audits of embedded systems and infrastructures for clients and partners.


    Beside my regular activities, I have the pleasure to teach cryptography and arithmetics as a teacher assistant since 2016 at the University of Technology of Troyes.

  • Enthusiast Traveler

    From Troyes, my hometown, to Atlanta to perfect my english skills, to Lund (Sweden) during my Erasmus semester where I decided to discover and experience different cultures, my way of life changed drastically. All my trips abroad are focused on the people way of living. Living with with the inhabitants in Sweden, I then moved on with three month of WWOOFing in a norwegian organic farm.


    I took this way of traveling to an extreme in 2015 when I decided to hitchhike across Europe just by myself and my tent. This trip drove me to Ukraine where people explained me the current state of war of the country around the Donbass Oblast, and then to Armenia where I discovered the Armenian-Azerbadji conflict. Despite the misery, I always found joy and hope in the youth who fought for their ideas.
